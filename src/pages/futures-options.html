Understanding Futures & Options

Futures and Options (F&O) are derivative instruments that derive their value from an underlying asset like stocks, commodities, or indices.

Types of Derivatives

Futures Contracts

Agreements to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price and date in the future.

Options Contracts

Give the right but not the obligation to buy (call) or sell (put) an asset at a specific price.

Key Components

  • Strike Price: The price at which the contract can be exercised
  • Premium: The price paid for the option contract
  • Expiration Date: The last day to exercise the contract
  • Lot Size: The minimum quantity that can be traded
  • Underlying Asset: The asset on which the derivative is based

Popular Trading Strategies

Hedging

Using derivatives to protect against price movements in the underlying asset.

Speculation

Trading based on market predictions to profit from price movements.

Arbitrage

Taking advantage of price differences between markets.

Risk Management
